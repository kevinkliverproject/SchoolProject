Climate Change

The impacts of the climate change are constantly increasing. 

Climate change is a slow phenomenon, manifesting over years and decades. 
It lacks the timeliness and punch of so many of today’s headliners and hot-button issues of concern. It is a uniquely difﬁcult challenge insofar as it requires people to act with urgency to prevent future scenarios that many ﬁnd unimaginable or even unbelievable in their present condition. As such, human interest in climate change has been slow to gain and difﬁcult to maintain.
Yet despite this challenge, public interest in and perception of climate change is critical. Without an informed public, governments, scientists and organizations will lack the social license necessary to act decisively to lower the impacts of this phenomen. 
Individuals who may wish to take action in their personal lives to lower their carbon footprint may lack the resources to do so or even the understanding that doing so is a decision that they could make.Public perception of the size of challenges associated with climate change is not where it needs to be. Studys in America also showed that many people believe that the climate change is not coming from the people.The global CO2 emissions have been steadily increasing and this has a considerable impact on the overheating of our planet. Since today the figures did not decrease enough to „save the planet“ although we are informed about the consequences of climate change.In addition, some people just don’t care and they don’t want to change their behaviour or use alternatives e.g. for the heat production. They think that a single person can’t change anything and that’s why they just keep doing what they do. And if many people feel and think like that, we can’t really save our planet from overheating.But there are still some things we can do to save our planet from overheating. Not all hope is gone and lost yet. In order to „save our planet“ we can use some alternatives in our daily life. Modernizing the house would be a first step into the right direction. Insulating the roof or putting new windows in, covering the pipes, installing new heating systems and solar power or filling the gaps around the door are ways to reduce energy and heating in an environmental friendly way. These are things that families and private households can do. The government should improve the public transport so that everyone is able to use e.g. the train instead of the car because it is a major cause of global warming. Car pollution contributes the to the climate change and that causes greenhouse gases. That means that if you try to travel less with the caror just dont it would have been a big change. Everyone can change something and if you don’t have the money to modernize your home or to avoid your car, you can do some little things and changes that will help too. You can start recycling more carefully or you can place low-energy lightbulbs around the house. Buying local food (vegetables, fruits,meat...) is also a good way to help the planet. Don’t throw rubbish into the nature, try to travel less in order to save CO2 emissions and create a better awareness for what’s going on and how global warming could affect our daily life if it doesn’t change.
